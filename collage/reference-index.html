famished

A hungry exploration into narrative through feminine themes and social issues with food and photography. The idea that strategy is stregthened with story and capable
of creating awareness in hopes to encourage conversation and awareness
for the power of change.

“C’mon honey! Not at the dinner table.”

Food scenes, taken from books, are styled and photographed in a series to explore the heart of overarching themes and the power of story. famishedis a visual study of feminine themes through photographs of food. This project aims to present feminine archetypes in an engaging way to keep create conversation and foster connection to recognize hierarchy in different forms everywhere and make change.

Note

A note on process: I came to this project from a tight turnaround deadline driven results based enviornment. I was all green lights go on the end result and top of mind answers. Process? Do you mean what can I have to you by the end of the day? So this project is a lot about process. It is about research, wrtitng, re-writing, circling back and making sense of the winding road. It is about returning to process.

Problem statment

Coming from a workplace with behavioral data driven strategy, I kept thinking about how we could bring more to the equation. I know that people have always told stories for a number of evolutionary reasons, one being the power of persuasion. Story is a strong part of branding for this reason. I think as a society we are coming to expect more from our media and stories as our consumption and eloquence in media rises. I think our work for media could carry more cues from a deeper narrative tone.

Currently, my work is in design, photography and food. Which really works out because I love food beyond just it’s ability to captivate the five senses. Food is at the heart of many stories and carries themes and nuance like a champ. It can be complex, layered, evocative and beautiful. It is also an elemental human need and experience. This project is a study in understanding the power of food and narrative through a series in photography.

Research

As a first step, I take the already beautifully constructed bones of a food scene from books or film and try to pull it apart for the themes and then put it back together for as a visual narrative feast. Through this practice, I try to understand the visual cues relevant to the themes and how to incorporate elements to better communicate meaning.

Much of my research began with film and film theory. I watched food scenes and read about their layered meanings and the hidden easter eggs to further convey nuance of the themes. I read lots of deconstruction and critique on thematic cues.

Process

For this project I needed an overarching theme as a through line for the essays. The more I researched and read I decided on feminine themes. It is personally resonant and something I can speak with authenticity about.

Early on in the project I planned on doing 3 photography essays including The Breakfast Club. Further in with the constraints of time and money, I narrowed it down to two. I found rich material in the books, Circe by Madeline Miller and The Bell Jar by Sylvia Plath. Once decided, I wrote and rewrote to try and find the essential focus for each story. Once the ideas were paired down, I wrote a breif,which I used as scaffolding. I created moodboards and loose storyboards to better illustrate my points. I thrifted for dishes and fabrics. I listed menus and food cues. I looked at lighting to unify the messaging through out the shoot. These were just the action points.

The biggest process challenges were the the questions that came up. A goal of Famished is to successfully create a resonant visual narrative that evokes connection and conversation. This came with big questions. What makes imagery/design universal? How do you determine the success of a narrative? What are metrics for empathy? How do you intend resonance? Is this all strategy?
